# Banking Churn Analysis & Retention Insights
 
## Case Study Overview

My Case study analyzes & handles 10k+ customer churn data for a bank to identify **high-risk segments**, **loyal customers**, and **growth opportunities**. Using SQL queries, this analysis provides **business-ready insights** for retention strategies, marketing campaigns, and customer engagement initiatives.
 
---
 
## Business Problems

1. **Country-Level Churn:** Which countries are losing the most customers?  

2. **Overall Churn & Tenure:** What percentage of customers leave after the average tenure?  

3. **Product Ownership & Churn:** How does the number of products affect customer loyalty?  

4. **High-Balance, Low-Credit Customers:** Who are the untapped marketing opportunities?  

5. **Multi-Product & Activity Status Churn:** Which segments are most at risk?  

6. **Gender-Based Churn:** How to ensure equitable retention initiatives across genders?
 
---
 
## Approach Strategies 

- **SQL Aggregation & Filtering:** Count, SUM, AVG, GROUP BY, RANK, and window functions  

- **Segmentation Analysis:** By geography, number of products, account balance, credit score, activity, and gender  

- **Business Interpretation:** Insights translated into actionable retention strategies  

- **Optional Visualization:** Dashboards or plots for executive-level presentation
 
---
 
## Key Insights
	
- **Country-Level Churn** - Germany loses the most customers compared to France and Spain 
	
- **Overall Churn % & Tenure** - 20.37% of customers leave after staying for the average tenure 

- **Product Ownership & Churn** - Customers with 2 products are the most loyal; 3-4 product customers show high churn 
	
- **High-Balance, Low-Credit Customers** - High-balance customers with credit scores <500 are prime targets for credit campaigns 
	
- **Multi-Product & Activity Status Churn** - Inactive multi-product customers have the highest churn risk 
	
- **Gender-Based Churn** - Analysis reveals gender-specific churn patterns, enabling inclusive retention programs 
  
---
 
## Actionable Recommendations

- **Retention Campaigns:** Target multi-product inactive customers with personalized offers  

- **Credit-Building Programs:** Engage high-balance, low-credit-score customers with credit products  

- **Gender-Inclusive Initiatives:** Design financial programs to improve retention for underrepresented groups  

- **Country-Focused Strategies:** Prioritize Germany for churn-reduction interventions  

- **Product Bundling Optimization:** Simplify offerings for 3-4 product customers to reduce churn
